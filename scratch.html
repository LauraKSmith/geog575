The Shifting Population of the Mississippi River Communities
Unlike the rest of the US populations along the mississippi river have been shrinking over time. Some towns are smaller because less trade takes place along the river, while others have noticable dips after natural disasters effect the area. Most of the southern cities suffered a dip in the 2010 census, when the area suffered severe flooding associated with Hurrican Katrina. Data is provided by the US Census, USGS, USDOT, FEMA, natural earthdata.com, and the Army Corp of Engineers.
